KoBar is a versatile platform designed to host a rich ecosystem of plugins, seamlessly integrating powerful tools directly into your daily workflow. As a core extension for this ecosystem, **Screenshot Studio** transforms KoBar into an advanced screen capture and annotation suite.

## What is Screenshot Studio?

A complete port of KoBar's native screen capturing capabilities, Screenshot Studio lets you grab pixel-perfect snips of any window or region across multiple monitors. It bypasses clunky OS snipping tools to bring screenshots straight into your KoBar clipboard queue, letting you immediately annotate, share, or save your captures without friction.

## Key Features

- **Multi-Monitor Support:** Seamlessly handles multi-display setups, allowing you to capture regions spanning across or specific to any active screen.
- **Precision Region Selection:** A lightweight, crosshair-based overlay enables you to drag and select the exact bounding box you need.
- **Rich Annotations Editor:** Once captured, instantly jump into the edit mode to draw shapes, highlight text, add arrows, or blur sensitive information.
- **Direct Clipboard Integration:** Bypasses manual saving—your captures and annotated edits are piped directly into KoBar's Multi-Slot Clipboard Manager, ready to be pasted sequentially.
- **Fast Exporting:** Quickly save annotated screenshots to your local file system or drag them straight into web applications.
- **Zero-Lag Overlay:** Uses a heavily optimized, transparent, borderless Electron window to ensure the selection process is stutter-free and accurate.

## Configuration & Usage

1. **Trigger Capture:** Click the Camera icon from the KoBar sidebar or use the global shortcut configured in your settings.
2. **Select Region:** Click and drag across the screen to define the capture area.
3. **Annotate:** The capture will open in the built-in Editor popup. Use the toolbar to draw, highlight, or redact.
4. **Save or Copy:** Click the "Copy to Slot" button to inject it into KoBar's clipboard, or click "Save" to export a `.png` file.
